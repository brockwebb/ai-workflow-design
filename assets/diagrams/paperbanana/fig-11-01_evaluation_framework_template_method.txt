# fig-11-01: Orchestration Evaluation Framework — Scoring Template

## Diagram Type
Table-style scoring template. Print-friendly. Designed as a reference tool the reader can fill in.

## Purpose
Operationalize the four-dimension evaluation framework from Chapter 11 as a concrete scoring tool. Three example rows pre-filled (one per orchestration category: managed platform, code-first framework, CLI/script-based). Two blank rows for the reader's own tools.

## Table Structure

### Header Row
| Tool / Approach | Coupling (1–5) | Friction (1–5) | Transparency (1–5) | Volatility (1–5) | Notes |

### Legend (displayed below or beside the table)
**Coupling:** 1 = fully decoupled (swap any component without rewrite) → 5 = tightly coupled (changes to one component require pipeline rewrite)
**Friction:** 1 = low overhead, fast setup, easy maintenance → 5 = high setup cost, significant maintenance burden, steep learning curve
**Transparency:** 1 = fully opaque (cannot audit what orchestration layer did) → 5 = fully transparent (every step logged, reproducible, inspectable)
**Volatility:** 1 = stable foundation, low deprecation risk → 5 = high churn, significant deprecation risk, unclear migration path

Note on scoring direction: higher scores indicate more risk for research pipelines. A tool with low coupling (1), low friction (1), high transparency (5), and low volatility (1) is the target profile.

### Pre-filled Example Rows

Row 1 — Managed Platform (representative example):
| Managed platform (e.g., low-code visual builder) | 4 | 2 | 2 | 3 | Fast to start; logic locked in platform; limited audit trail; viable if provenance not required |

Row 2 — Code-first Framework (representative example):
| Code-first framework (e.g., active LLM framework) | 3 | 3 | 3 | 4 | Powerful; moderate coupling via abstractions; transparency depends on implementation; high churn risk |

Row 3 — CLI / Script-based:
| CLI / script-based (Python + bash + config) | 1 | 3 | 5 | 1 | Maximum transparency and control; front-loaded investment; stable foundations; recommended for audit requirements |

## Visual Style
- Clean, high-contrast, print-safe
- Header row: dark gray fill (#333333), white text
- Legend section: light gray background (#F2F2F2), smaller text
- Pre-filled rows: alternating white and very light gray (#FAFAFA)
- Blank rows: white fill with dashed bottom border to signal "fill in here"
- Score cells in pre-filled rows: plain number, center-aligned
- Blank score cells: underscore or light dotted box to invite handwriting
- Column widths: Tool column widest (~35%); four score columns equal width (~10% each); Notes column remainder
- No color coding for scores — deliberately neutral so reader applies their own judgment
- Table border: thin dark gray (#333333)
- Font: sans-serif (Inter, Helvetica, or similar)

## Accompanying Note (caption or sidebar)
"Apply this framework before adoption, not after. Rate each dimension for your candidate tool, then compare profiles. A research pipeline with audit requirements needs low coupling, high transparency, and low volatility. The friction score is a practical constraint, not a quality judgment — a high-friction tool may still be the right choice if the other dimensions are favorable."

## What This Is NOT
- Not a product comparison or endorsement of any specific tool
- Not a prescriptive decision tree — the framework produces a profile, not a verdict
- The pre-filled examples are representative of categories, not reviews of specific products
- Score thresholds ("good" vs. "bad") are intentionally absent — context determines what scores are acceptable
