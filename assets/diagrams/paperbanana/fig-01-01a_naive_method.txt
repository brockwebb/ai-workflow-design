# fig-01-01a: The Naive Workflow

## Diagram Type
Technical block diagram. Clean, minimal, professional.

## Layout
Horizontal left-to-right flow. Three blocks connected by arrows.

## Elements
1. Rectangle labeled "Input" — left side
2. Arrow pointing right
3. Rectangle labeled "Model" — center
4. Arrow pointing right
5. Rectangle labeled "Output" — right side

## Visual Style
- Clean, sparse, deliberately simple
- Muted colors: light gray fills, dark gray or black outlines and text
- No decorative elements, no gradients, no shadows
- The simplicity IS the point — this diagram looks deceptively easy
- Sans-serif font (e.g., Inter, Helvetica, or similar clean typeface)
- Generous whitespace around all elements
- Arrows are simple, thin, with small arrowheads

## Color Palette
- Background: white or very light warm gray (#F8F7F5)
- Box fill: light gray (#E8E8E8)
- Box outline: dark gray (#333333)
- Text: black (#1A1A1A)
- Arrows: dark gray (#333333)

## Dimensions
- Landscape orientation, roughly 4:1 aspect ratio (wide and short)
- Suitable for 80% page width in a book layout

## What This Is NOT
- Not busy or complex
- Not colorful
- Not labeled with technical jargon
- No annotations, callouts, or footnotes
- No icons or symbols inside the boxes

## Caption
"The naive workflow. Input goes in, model processes it, output comes out."
